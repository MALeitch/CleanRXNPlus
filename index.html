How Clean RXN+ Works
①
Select in ChemDraw
Highlight your entire reaction scheme — reactants, products, arrows, and any above/below labels — then the panel parses the CDXML automatically.
②
Inspect the Board
Each molecule and label appears as a draggable card. Columns represent spatial zones (Reactants → Step N → Products). Drag cards to reorder, merge, or move them to the Holding Area.
③
Tune the Layout
Use the spacing slider and stack gap to control horizontal and vertical spacing. Toggle H-Respect on individual cards to auto-nudge them away from overlapping neighbors. Use the arrow column tools for reagent label placement.
④
Apply to ChemDraw
Click Apply to ChemDraw ↩ to write the computed dx/dy offsets back to the canvas. The preview shows exactly what you'll get before committing. Use ⚡ Auto-Layout (Ctrl+Shift+X) for a one-click clean without opening the board.
Key Concepts
Center card The anchor molecule in each column — marked with a green left border. All other cards in the column stack above or below it. Click "Set as Center" on any card to reassign the anchor.
H-Respect When enabled on a card, the layout engine nudges it vertically to prevent it from overlapping cards in neighboring columns. Useful for large reagent labels beside compact molecules.
Balance above/below On mol columns, distributes the full stack evenly above and below the arrow centerline instead of anchoring to a specific card.
Holding Area A parking zone for cards you want to exclude from layout temporarily. Held cards are not moved when Apply is clicked.
Presets Save your favorite spacing and mode settings by name and recall them with one click across sessions.
